The Wonderful Life of Alan Turing

English mathematician, computer scientist, logician, cryptanalyst, philosopher, and theoretical biologist. (1912 - 1954)

  • Major contributor to the theory of computation. Famous for the "Turing Machine".
  • During World War II, he served as a codebreaker for the UK. His contributions created a major positive impact in the war effort, via intercepting critical bits of military intelligence, which ulitimately resulted in a victory for the Allied Powers.
  • Persecuted for being homosexual. He continues to serve as an inspiration for computer scientists and the LGBTQ+ community alike.
